Gamma Seeker

Spot + H3D Compton camera for autonomous gamma-source localization

Overview

Integrated an H3D M400 Compton camera with Boston Dynamics Spot to autonomously localize a gamma source. The system fuses Compton imaging with LiDAR-based mapping and navigates to areas of highest likelihood.

Build Process

  1. Hardware Mounting & Power: Designed/secured a vibration-aware mount; provided isolated power and cable management.
  2. Networking: Unified time-sync and low-latency comms between Spot and the Compton camera host.
  3. ROS 2 Drivers: Wrote/modified nodes to acquire list-mode/processed counts and publish Compton cones.
  4. Sensor Fusion: Registered Compton cones into the LiDAR frame; accumulated likelihood on a 2D/3D grid.
  5. Autonomy Loop: Selected goal cells with highest expected information gain; iterated measure → move → update.
  6. Field Validation: Ran controlled-source trials; logged rates, dwell times, and trajectory data.

Results

  • Consistently converged near the source with a clear likelihood peak.
  • Operated fully on-robot with repeatable runs and logged metrics.
  • Produced intuitive heatmaps for operator review and after-action analysis.
