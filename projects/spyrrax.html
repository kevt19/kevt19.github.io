Spyrrax

A scratch-built 4-legged spider robot

Overview

Spyrrax is a custom 4-legged platform exploring legged locomotion, joint control, and gait generation on a budget-friendly stack.

Build Process

  1. Mechanics: CAD design for hip/knee joints; 3D-printed links; hardware selection for torque/weight tradeoffs.
  2. Actuation: Hobby servos/Dynamixels (as available); bracket stiffness and horn alignment for repeatability.
  3. Electronics: Controller (e.g., Teensy/ESP32), 2S/3S battery, buck/boost regulation, wiring harness.
  4. Firmware: Joint angle commands, trajectory interpolation, and safety stops.
  5. Gait Control: Tripod/crawl gaits; phase offsets; simple body stabilization.
  6. Tuning: PID gains and mechanical backlash compensation; thermal/power checks.

Results

  • Stable crawl and basic turning maneuvers over flat terrain.
  • Modular design enables quick leg/servo swaps and future sensors.
  • Clear path to improved speed via higher-torque actuators and state estimation.
