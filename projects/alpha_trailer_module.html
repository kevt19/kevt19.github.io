Alpha Trailer Module

Air-proportional detector on a diff-drive robot for autonomous alpha detection

Overview

A tow-behind module housing an air-proportional detector, integrated with a differential-drive robot for autonomous area sweeps and hotspot detection of alpha-emitting materials.

Build Process

  1. Mechanical: Designed a stable, low-CG trailer; sealed detector enclosure with airflow path.
  2. Electronics: Signal conditioning, bias supply, and data acquisition to single-board computer.
  3. ROS Integration: Drivers for count rate; time-stamped messages; map overlays.
  4. Coverage Planner: Lane/spiral sweeps with adjustable dwell times.
  5. Hotspot Logic: Thresholds with temporal filtering to avoid false positives.
  6. Field Tests: Calibration passes; background subtraction; log & replay tooling.

Results

  • Detected elevated alpha counts with clear spatial correlation.
  • Produced easy-to-read sweeps and hotspot markers for operators.
  • Trailer dynamics had minimal impact on base navigation after tuning.
